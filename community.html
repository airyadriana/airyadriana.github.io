Urban Native Gardens & Habitat Connectivity

Community Science Urban Ecology GIS Mapping

Community-led conservation combining hands-on native pollinator garden installations with spatial analysis for Friends of Coal Creek. I lead garden projects within my homeowners association while mapping certified gardens throughout the Coal Creek watershed.

Community Native Garden Installation

Volunteers planting native wildflowers and grasses in the Coal Creek watershed. These gardens provide critical nectar and host plants for local pollinators including native bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds. Each garden installation is documented and added to our connectivity mapping project.

GIS Analysis of Garden Networks

Spatial analysis showing certified native gardens throughout the Coal Creek watershed. Each point represents a Pollinator City certified garden, creating stepping-stone habitat corridors that allow wildlife to move through suburban landscapes. Distance analysis reveals which neighborhoods have the strongest connectivity and where future gardens would have the greatest conservation impact.

Before: Traditional Lawn

A typical suburban lawn dominated by non-native Kentucky bluegrass. While aesthetically pleasing to some, these monocultures provide minimal value for native wildlife and require significant water, fertilizer, and maintenance.

After: Native Pollinator Garden

Pollinator City Tracking Dashboard

Interactive ArcGIS dashboard tracking certification progress across the watershed. The tool allows volunteers and city planners to visualize garden density, identify gaps in habitat connectivity, and prioritize neighborhoods for future outreach efforts. Data includes garden size, plant species lists, and maintenance schedules.
