Airy Peralta

Ecologist | Climate Researcher | Pika Research Project

Born and raised in Mexico, currently living in Colorado and participating in the Pika Research Project at the University of Colorado Boulder.

My Journey

My path to ecology began in Mexico, where I developed a deep connection with nature and a curiosity about the intricate relationships between organisms and their environments. This early passion led me to pursue research in climate science and wildlife ecology.

Current Research

I focus on understanding how microclimatic temperatures affect ecological systems, particularly in alpine environments. My work at the University of Colorado Boulder involves the Pika Research Project, where I study these small mammals that serve as indicators of climate change.

At the intersection of field ecology and computational science, I use statistical modeling and data analysis to understand climate impacts on wildlife habitats. Through projects studying talus slopes and pika territories, I aim to contribute to our understanding of how small-scale temperature variations influence species distribution and habitat suitability in a changing climate.

Research Interests

My work spans several interconnected areas: microclimatic temperature modeling, alpine ecosystem dynamics, climate change impacts on wildlife, statistical analysis in ecology, and community-based conservation. Each project builds on my commitment to understanding and preserving mountain ecosystems.

Philosophy

I believe that understanding the smallest details of how organisms interact with their thermal environment can reveal broader patterns about ecosystem resilience and adaptation to climate change. Field work grounds my research in reality, while computational tools allow me to see patterns across space and time.
